Implications

Healthcare spending consists of 1/6 of the US Economy, yet there are massive treatment gaps that still exist. Treatment gaps present business opportunities and cases for decreasing costs to insurers and government-subsidized programs, as well as revenue streams for service providers.

The Market Opportunity

→ Say there is only 1% prevalence of OUD in a state. How many patients need treatment?

  • Maryland: 66% (40,000)
  • Ohio: 82% (90,000)
  • Texas: 95% (270,000)

→ How much does it cost to treat OUD? $5,000/yr - Methadone, $10,000/yr - Buprenorphine.

Recommendation: Expand Buprenorphine-based Treatment

→ Patient Preference + Massive Market

→ High Reimbursement

→ Rapid Deployment: Flexible Business Model

→ Excellent choice for rural and urban patients

Recommendation: Expand Treatment Service Value Chain

→ Increase Number of Addiction Therapists

→ Create Digital Tools: Symptomatology, EHRs/platforms specializing in addiction/mental health, daily-dosing applications

→ Drug Testing: locations & kits

→ Pharmacies: stock buprenorphine-products, anti-nausea, sleep aids, expand into drug testing services, etc.


Winners and Losers

Loser→ Insurers

Mixed→ Medicaid: short and medium term financial losses, long term gains

Loser→ Methadone Clinics that do not expand into buprenorphine

Loser→ The "Methadone Clinic business model": Dispensing Windows

Loser→ Cash Pay Treatment Clinics

Winner→ Generic buprenorphine-based drug manufacturers
